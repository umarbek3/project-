About Me

Cristiano Ronaldo was born on February 5, 1985, on the island of Madeira, Portugal. He grew up in a humble background and was raised by his mother, who worked as a cook, and his father, who was a kit man at a local football club. Ronaldo began playing football at a young age, and it quickly became evident that he had a talent for the game. At the age of 8, Ronaldo joined a local football club called Andorinha. He quickly stood out as a talented player and was soon scouted by the biggest club on the island, Nacional. Ronaldo's father was hesitant to let him go, but his mother convinced him to allow Cristiano to pursue his dreams. In 1997, at the age of 12, Ronaldo left his family and moved to the mainland of Portugal to join the youth academy of Sporting CP. It was a difficult time for Ronaldo, as he was far away from his family and struggled to adapt to life in a new city. However, his talent on the pitch soon caught the attention of the coaches at Sporting CP. After a few years in the youth academy, Ronaldo made his professional debut for Sporting CP against Moreirense. He was just 18 years old at the time and was given the number 7 jersey, previously worn by United legends such as George Best and Eric Cantona. Ronaldo's first season at Manchester United was a tough one. He struggled to adapt to the physicality of the English game and received criticism from the media for his diving and theatrics on the pitch. However, he soon found his feet and began to showcase his incredible skills. In the 2006-2007 season, Ronaldo was a key player in Manchester United's success.
